VECTORS

Applications of Dot & Cross Products

Work (in physics) is done whenever a force causes linear displacement

Torque is the turning effect done by a force that causes angular displacement

Work
\( W = \vec{F}\cdot\vec{d} \)
\( W = \vert\vec{F}\vert\vert\vec{d}\vert\cos{\theta} \)
Unit is \( N\cdot m \) or Joules
Area of llgm
\( Area = l \cdot w \)
\( Area = \vert\vec{a}\vert\vert\vec{b}\vert\sin{\theta} \)
\( Area = \vert\vec{a} \times \vec{b}\vert \)
Torque
\( \vec{T} = \vec{r} \times \vec{F} \)
\( \vec{T} = \vert\vec{r}\vert\vert\vec{F}\vert\sin{\theta} \text{ } \hat{n} \)
Unit is \( N\cdot m \) or Joules

Find the area of the triangle with the vertices P(7,2,-5), Q(9,-1,-6), and R(7,3,-3)

Use area of llgm formula but cut it in half.

\( \vec{PQ} = (9-7, -1-2, -6--5) = (2, -3, -1) \)
\( \vec{PR} = (7-7, 3-2, -3--5) = (0, 1, 2) \)
\( \vec{PQ} \times \vec{PR} = (-6+1, 0-4, 2-0) = (-5, -4, 2) \)
\( \therefore \text{Area of triangle} = \dfrac{\vert \vec{PQ} \times \vec{PR} \vert}{2} \)
\( = \dfrac{\sqrt{5^2 + 4^2 + 2^2}}{2} = \dfrac{3\sqrt{5}}{2}\)

A 25kg box is located 8m up a ramp inclined at an angle of 18° to the horizontal. Determine the work done by the force of gravity as the box slides to the bottom of the ramp.

\( W = \vec{F} \cdot \vec{d} \)
\( = \vert\vec{F}\vert\vert\vec{d}\vert\cos{\theta} \)
\( = (245)(8)cos{72°} \)
\( = 606 \text{ Nm or Joules} \)

Determine the minimum force, acting at an angle of 40° to the horizontal, required to slide the box back up the ramp (Ignore friction).

Break both \( \vec{F_g} \) and \( \vec{F} \) forces into components ensuring they are parallel to the ramp. Now for the box to move, the up component needs to be slightly bigger than the down component.

Use CAH (Cosine = Adjacent over Hypotenuse).

\( \vert\vec{F}\vert \cos{22°} > \vert\vec{F_g}\vert \cos{72} \)
\( \vert\vec{F}\vert > \dfrac{245\cos{72°}}{\cos{22°}} \)
\( \vert\vec{F}\vert > 81.7 \text{ N } \)

Find the torque produced by a cyclist exterting a force of 115 N on a pedal in the position shown in the diagram, if the shaft of the pedal is 16cm long.

\( \vec{T} = \vec{r} \times \vec{F} \)
\( \vec{T} = \vert\vec{r}\vert\vert\vec{F}\vert \sin{\theta} \text{ } \hat{n} \)
\( \vec{T} = (0.16)(115) \sin{100°} \text{ } \hat{n} \)
\( \vec{T} = \text{ 18.1 Joules [into the page] } \)
